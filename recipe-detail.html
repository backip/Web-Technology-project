Nepali Mo:Mo

    Here is incrediabally easy to follow recipe for Nepali Mo:Mo
    Ingredients:
    For the Momo Dough:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • A pinch of salt
  • Water (for kneading)
    • For the Chicken Filling:
  • 1/2 lb ground chicken
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped cabbage
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped cilantro
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1-2 tablespoons cooking oil
    • For the Momo Sauce (Dipping Sauce):
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chili sauce (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon vinegar
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
    • Instructions: 1. Prepare the Momo Dough:
  • In a large bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and a pinch of salt.
  • Gradually add water and knead the dough until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  • Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
    • 2. Prepare the Chicken Filling:
  • In a pan, heat 1-2 tablespoons of cooking oil over medium heat.
  • Add minced garlic and ginger, sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  • Add finely chopped onions and cook until they become translucent.
  • Add ground chicken and cook until it turns white and crumbly.
  • Stir in turmeric, cumin, coriander powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  • Add chopped cabbage and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  • Finally, add chopped cilantro, mix, and remove from heat. Let the filling cool.
    • 3. Assemble the Momo:
  • Take a small portion of the dough and roll it into a small, thin circle (about 3 inches in diameter).
  • Place a spoonful of the chicken filling in the center of the dough circle.
  • Fold the circle in half to create a half-moon shape.
  • Pinch the edges together to seal the momo. You can use various folding techniques for a decorative edge.
    • 4. Steam the Momo:
  • Grease a steamer tray to prevent sticking.
  • Place the momo on the steamer tray, leaving some space between them.
  • Steam the momo for about 15-20 minutes until the dough becomes translucent and the filling is cooked through.
    • 5. Prepare the Momo Sauce:
  • While the momo are steaming, mix soy sauce, chili sauce, sesame oil, vinegar, and minced garlic in a small bowl. Adjust the sauce to your taste.
    • 6. Serve:
  • Once the momo are cooked, remove them from the steamer.
  • Serve hot with the dipping sauce on the side.
Enjoy your homemade Nepali chicken momo! You can also experiment with different fillings like vegetable, pork, or beef to suit your preferences.

Chicken Briyani

Ingredients:

  • For Marinating Chicken:
    • 500 grams (1 pound) chicken, cut into pieces
    • 1 cup yogurt
    • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
    • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1 teaspoon garam masala
    • Salt to taste
  • For Rice:
    • 2 cups Basmati rice
    • 4 cups water
    • 2-3 green cardamom pods
    • 2-3 cloves
    • 1 bay leaf
    • Salt to taste
  • For Biryani:
    • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
    • 2 tomatoes, chopped
    • 2 green chilies, slit
    • 1/4 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
    • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
    • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil or ghee
    • 1/4 cup warm milk
    • A pinch of saffron strands (optional, for garnish)
    • Ghee for frying onions (optional)
    • Fried onions for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Marinating the Chicken:
    • In a large mixing bowl, combine the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt.
    • Add the chicken pieces to the marinade mixture. Ensure the chicken is well-coated with the marinade. Cover the bowl and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes. You can refrigerate it for a few hours for better flavor.
  2. Preparing the Rice:
    • Wash the Basmati rice thoroughly under cold running water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes, then drain.
    • In a large pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add the drained rice, green cardamom pods, cloves, bay leaf, and a pinch of salt. Cook until the rice is 70-80% cooked, with a slight bite to it. Drain the rice and set it aside.
  3. Making the Biryani:
    • Heat vegetable oil or ghee in a large, heavy-bottomed pan or a biryani pot over medium heat. If desired, you can also fry the thinly sliced onions in ghee until they are brown and crispy for garnish.
    • Add the sliced onions to the hot oil and sauté until they turn golden brown. Remove half of the fried onions and set them aside for garnish.
    • Add the marinated chicken to the remaining onions in the pot. Cook the chicken on medium heat until it's no longer pink, and the oil begins to separate from the masala. Stir occasionally.
    • Add the chopped tomatoes and green chilies. Cook until the tomatoes become soft and the oil separates again.
    • Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken mixture in the pot. Sprinkle half of the chopped coriander and mint leaves on top.
    • Drizzle warm milk over the rice and sprinkle saffron strands (if using) for added flavor and color.
    • Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and reduce the heat to low. Let it cook for 20-25 minutes or until the chicken is tender and the rice is fully cooked and aromatic.
    • Garnish the chicken biryani with the reserved fried onions, coriander leaves, and mint leaves.
    • Serve hot with raita (yogurt sauce) or a side salad.

Egg Fried Rice

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked and cooled white rice (preferably day-old rice)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onion
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped carrots
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas, thawed
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-3 tablespoons soy sauce (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Green onions (scallions), chopped for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Ingredients:
    • Cook the rice ahead of time and let it cool. Day-old rice works best for fried rice as it's drier and less likely to become mushy.
    • Beat the eggs in a small bowl and set them aside.
    • Finely chop the onion, carrots, and garlic. Thaw the frozen peas.
  2. Cook the Eggs:
    • Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
    • Pour the beaten eggs into the skillet and cook them, stirring gently, until they are scrambled and just set.
    • Remove the scrambled eggs from the skillet and set them aside.
  3. Sauté the Vegetables:
    • In the same skillet, add another tablespoon of oil if needed.
    • Add the minced garlic and chopped onion. Sauté for a minute or two until they become fragrant and the onion becomes translucent.
    • Add the finely chopped carrots and cook for 3-4 minutes until they start to soften.
    • Stir in the thawed peas and cook for another 2 minutes.
  4. Add Rice and Eggs:
    • Add the cooked and cooled rice to the skillet. Break up any clumps and stir-fry with the vegetables for a few minutes until the rice is heated through.
    • Return the scrambled eggs to the skillet and mix them with the rice and vegetables.
  5. Season with Soy Sauce:
    • Drizzle 2-3 tablespoons of soy sauce evenly over the rice mixture. You can adjust the amount of soy sauce to your taste.
    • Season with salt and pepper as needed.
  6. Finish and Serve:
    • Continue to stir-fry the fried rice for a few more minutes until everything is well combined and heated through.
    • Optionally, garnish with chopped green onions (scallions).
    • Serve your homemade Egg Fried Rice hot as a side dish or a main course.

Enjoy your delicious homemade Egg Fried Rice! Feel free to customize it by adding other ingredients like cooked chicken, shrimp, or your favorite vegetables.

Samosa

Ingredients:

Ingredients:

  • For the Samosa Dough:
    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1/4 cup vegetable oil or ghee (clarified butter)
    • A pinch of salt
    • Water (for kneading)
  • For the Samosa Filling:
    • 2 large potatoes, boiled, peeled, and chopped into small cubes
    • 1/2 cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
    • 1 small onion, finely chopped
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
    • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
    • Salt to taste
    • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
    • Fresh cilantro leaves, chopped (optional, for garnish)
  • For Frying:
    • Vegetable oil (for deep frying)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Samosa Dough:
    • In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, a pinch of salt, and 1/4 cup of vegetable oil or ghee.
    • Gradually add water and knead the mixture into a firm and smooth dough. Cover it with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Samosa Filling:
    • In a pan, heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil over medium heat.
    • Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds.
    • Add minced garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Sauté for a minute until fragrant.
    • Add chopped onions and cook until they become translucent.
    • Stir in coriander powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt.
    • Add the cubed potatoes and green peas. Mix well and cook for 5-7 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld. Remove from heat and let the filling cool.
  3. Shape the Samosas:
    • Divide the rested dough into small equal portions and roll each portion into a ball.
    • Roll out each ball into a thin oval or round shape, about 6 inches in diameter.
    • Cut each oval in half to form two semi-circles.
    • Take one semi-circle and fold it in half to create a cone shape.
    • Seal the edges using a little water to form a cone.
    • Fill the cone with the potato-pea filling, about 2-3 tablespoons.
    • Seal the open edge, pinching it tightly to ensure the filling doesn't spill out.
  4. Fry the Samosas:
    • Heat vegetable oil in a deep frying pan over medium-high heat.
    • Carefully slide the prepared samosas into the hot oil, a few at a time.
    • Fry them until they turn golden brown and crispy, turning occasionally. This usually takes about 6-8 minutes.
    • Remove the fried samosas using a slotted spoon and place them on paper towels to drain excess oil.
  5. Serve:
    • Serve hot samosas with mint chutney, tamarind sauce, or ketchup.
    • Optionally, garnish with chopped fresh cilantro leaves.

Enjoy your homemade samosas as a delightful snack or appetizer!

Lassi

Ingredients:

  1. 1 cup plain yogurt (preferably full-fat)
  2. 1/2 cup cold water
  3. 3-4 tablespoons granulated sugar (adjust to taste)
  4. 1/2 teaspoon ground cardamom (optional)
  5. A few ice cubes (optional)
Instructions:
  1. Prepare the Sweet Lassi:
    • In a blender, combine 1 cup of plain yogurt, 1/2 cup of cold water, and 3-4 tablespoons of granulated sugar. You can adjust the sugar to your preferred sweetness level.
    • If you like, add a pinch of ground cardamom for extra flavor.
    • Blend the mixture until it's smooth and frothy.
  2. Serve:
    • If you like your lassi chilled, you can add a few ice cubes to the blender and blend them with the mixture for a colder beverage.
    • Pour the lassi into glasses.
    • Optionally, garnish with a sprinkle of ground cardamom or a few slivers of almonds or pistachios.
  3. Enjoy:
    • Serve your sweet or mango lassi immediately while it's cold and refreshing.

Lassi is a versatile drink, and you can adjust the sweetness and consistency to your liking by varying the amount of sugar and water. It's perfect for enjoying on a hot day or as a complement to spicy Indian dishes.
